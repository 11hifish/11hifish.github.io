About Me

As an undergrad at Carnegie Mellon University, I enjoy exploring researches and courses in data science and machine learning. I did researches in statistical machine learning, both theory and application (in FinTech and database systems), as well as reinforcement learning (autonomous driving). I like taking courses in general machine learning, deep reinforcement learning and convex optimization.


Past course works, research and industrial experiences make me intrigued by the power of machine learning as interpreting data into vivid, useful stories. My research interest is in how people can efficiently leverage and interpret data to solve real world problems or make better decisions. I especially enjoy the process of discovering problems from real world, formulating the problems in a generalized version, developing new algorithms and applying them back to the real world in a larger domain.
